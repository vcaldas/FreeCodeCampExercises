Life events

  • October 3, 1954 - Stevie Ray Vaughan was born.
  • 1964 - Stevie Ray Vaughan was born.
  • 1975 - When Stevie was ten, we played his first guitar, inspired by the playings of his older brother, Jimmie Vaughan. Triple Threat was formed, which was renamed "Double Trouble".
  • 1979 - Married Lenora Darlene Bailey.
  • 1982 - Mick Jagger invited Double Trouble to New York City.
  • 1983 - After his first record "Texas Flood" went out, Stevie Ray Vaughan was voted "Beat New Talent" and "Best Electric Blues Guitarist" by Guitar Player Magazine.
  • 1984 - Stevie Ray Vaughan was unexpectedly recognized by the National Blues Foundation Awards, who voted him Entertainer of the Year and Blues Instrumentalist of the Year.
  • 1985 - Stevie's second record, "Couldn't Stand the Weather", climbed to No. 31 on the charts and went gold.
  • 1986 - Due to his bad cocaine and alcohol problem, Stevie Ray Vaughan collapsed during a concert in Europe.
  • 1989 - Double Trouble's fourth album "In Step"was released.
  • 1990 - After a concert in Wisconsin, Stevie went on a helicopter that was on its way to Chicago. Due to dense fog, the helicopter piolet couldn't see, and crashed, killing everyone inside.
